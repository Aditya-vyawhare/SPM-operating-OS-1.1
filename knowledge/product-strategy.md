# Dice Product Strategy for GMV Growth

## North star

Increase quarterly GMV with healthy monetization quality (take rate and contribution margin), not growth at any cost.

## Strategic pillars

## 1) Increase conversion

- Improve fare freshness and pricing consistency at search and checkout
- Reduce booking friction in policy-aware flows for corporate travelers
- Strengthen trust signals: refund rules, fare guarantees, and transparent fees

Target impact: +6% to +10% booking conversion.

## 2) Increase AOV

- Promote higher-value itineraries with better utility (timings, fewer layovers)
- Expand ancillary bundles (seat + meal + baggage + flex change)
- Introduce smart default bundles for frequent business routes

Target impact: +5% to +8% AOV.

## 3) Improve retention and repeat

- Focus on company-level repeat booking via policy personalization
- Build proactive disruption handling (rebooking and traveler support)
- Launch account-level travel savings and spend intelligence

Target impact: +4% to +7% repeat rate.

## 4) Reduce leakage and protect realized GMV

- Reconcile supplier fare, refunds, and ancillaries within 24 hours
- Flag mismatches before settlement and automate exception workflows
- Tighten GST/tax validation and invoice reconciliation controls

Target impact: recover 1.5% to 3.0% of GMV currently leaking.

## Prioritization lens

Prioritize initiatives scoring high on:

- GMV upside in 2 quarters
- Speed to measurable impact
- Low-to-medium implementation effort
- Low compliance/operational risk
- Reliable instrumentation for causal measurement

## Example portfolio mix (next 2 quarters)

- 50% funnel and pricing reliability
- 30% leakage detection and reconciliation automation
- 20% retention and account expansion bets
