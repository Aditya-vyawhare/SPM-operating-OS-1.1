# Dice Business Model (Corporate Travel)

Dice is a corporate travel platform monetizing business travel across flights, hotels, bus, and train bookings.

## Revenue streams

- **Supplier commissions:** airline, hotel, and rail commissions captured per booking
- **Markups:** spread between net and displayed fare/hotel rates
- **Service fees:** booking, reissue, cancellation, and offline support fees
- **Ancillary monetization:** seats, meals, baggage, insurance, priority services

## GMV definition

**GMV = Total gross booking value confirmed in period before cancellations and refunds.**

For operating reviews, track:

- Gross GMV (all confirmed bookings)
- Net GMV (gross GMV minus cancellations/refunds)
- Realized GMV (net GMV with reconciled supplier pricing)

## Core GMV drivers

- Booking volume (active companies x travelers x trips per traveler)
- Conversion rate by search-to-book funnel stage
- Average order value (AOV) by mode and route mix
- Ancillary attach rate
- Cancellation and refund leakage control
- Supply quality (fare freshness, inventory depth, preferred content)

## Unit economics framework

At booking level:

- **Revenue per booking** = commission + markup + service fees + ancillary margin
- **Variable cost per booking** = payment gateway + support handling + fulfillment cost
- **Contribution per booking** = revenue per booking - variable cost per booking

Portfolio level:

- Take rate = total revenue / GMV
- Contribution margin = contribution / GMV
- Leakage rate = unrecovered value / GMV

## Baseline operating assumption

- Current volume: ~800 bookings/day
- Primary constraint: revenue leakage and reconciliation delays
- Immediate focus: increase realized GMV, not only gross GMV
