# Customer Segments (Dice Corporate Travel)

## 1) Corporate Employees (Bookers and Travelers)

Who they are:

- Employees booking business travel for self-serve or approved trips

What they value:

- Fast booking, reliable fares, clear policy eligibility, and easy changes

GMV levers:

- Higher conversion with fewer checkout failures
- Higher AOV via recommended itineraries and ancillaries
- Higher repeat through dependable support and disruption handling

Key friction points:

- Fare changes at checkout
- Confusing policy restrictions
- Slow refund and reissue flows

## 2) Travel Admins

Who they are:

- Company administrators managing policy, approvals, and travel budgets

What they value:

- Policy control, spend visibility, duty of care, and reduced manual work

GMV levers:

- Enables broader adoption inside enterprise accounts
- Improves approval velocity and prevents booking drop-off
- Supports negotiated supply usage and preferred rates

Key friction points:

- Manual policy exceptions
- Weak reporting on policy adherence and savings
- Limited control over out-of-policy leakage

## 3) Finance Teams

Who they are:

- AP and finance operations teams handling invoices, refunds, tax, and reconciliation

What they value:

- Accurate invoices, fast month-end close, auditable trails, and reduced leakage

GMV levers:

- Protects realized GMV by reducing refund and fare mismatch leakage
- Improves trust and retention for enterprise accounts
- Reduces revenue slippage from reconciliation delays

Key friction points:

- Manual reconciliation across booking, supplier, and payment systems
- GST/tax mismatch corrections
- Delayed or missing refund recovery
